II. For Monks and Religious: A. For an Abbot

Entrance Antiphon | Cf. Ps 92 (91): 13-14

The just will flourish like the palm tree, and grow like a Lebanon cedar, planted in the house of the LORD, in the courts of the house of our God (alleluia).

or: | Ps 37 (36): 30-31

The mouth of the just man utters wisdom, and his tongue tells forth what is right. The law of his God is in his heart (alleluia).

>> Introductory Rites and Penitential Act [Sample]

Collect

Let us pray.

And all pray in silence with the Priest for a while.

Grant us, O Lord,
that amid the uncertainties of this world
we may cling with all our heart to the things of heaven,
for through the Abbot blessed N.
you have given us a model of evangelical perfection.

Through our Lord Jesus Christ, your Son,
who lives and reigns with you in the unity of the Holy Spirit,
one God, for ever and ever.

>> Liturgy of the Eucharist (Preparation of the Gifts)

Prayer over the Offerings

Grant those who approach your altar,
O Lord, that spirit of devotion
with which the blessed Abbot N. was on fire,
so that, pure of heart and fervent in charity,
we may offer you a worthy sacrifice.
Through Christ our Lord.

Preface of Saints I or Preface of Saints II

Communion Antiphon | Cf. Lk 12: 42

This is the steward, faithful and prudent, whom the Lord set over his household, to give them their allowance of food at the proper time (alleluia).

or: | Mt 23: 11

The greatest among you must be your servant, says the Lord (alleluia).

Prayer after Communion

By the power of this Sacrament which we have received,
renew our hearts, O Lord,
so that by the example of the Abbot blessed N.,
being wise in the things above
and not in the things of earth here below,
we may merit to appear in glory with Christ.
Who lives and reigns for ever and ever.
