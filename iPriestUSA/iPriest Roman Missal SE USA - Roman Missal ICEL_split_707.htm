26. FOR THE SANCTIFICATION OF HUMAN LABOR

Option A | Option B

Entrance Antiphon | Gen 1:1, 27, 31

In the beginning, when God created the heavens and the earth,  God created man in his image; God looked at everything he had made,  and he found it very good.

or: | Cf. Ps 89:17

May your favour, O Lord, be upon us. And may you give success to the work of our hands.

>> Introductory Rites and Penitential Act [Sample]

Collect

Let us pray.

And all pray in silence with the Priest for a while.

God, Creator of all things,
who have commanded the human race to bear the burden of labor,
grant that the work we are beginning may bring progress in this life
and, by your favour, advance the spread of Christ’s Kingdom.

Who lives and reigns with you.

Or:

God,
who through human labor never cease to perfect
and govern the vast work of creation,
listen to the supplications of your people
and grant that all men and women
may find work that befits their dignity,
joins them more closely to one another
and enables them to serve their neighbour.
Through our Lord.

Other prayers

>> Liturgy of the Eucharist (Preparation of the Gifts)

Prayer over the Offerings

O God, who in the gifts presented here
nourish the human race with food
and renew it with your Sacrament;
grant, we pray, that the sustenance they impart
may not fail us in body or in mind.
Through Christ our Lord.

Preface V of the Sundays in Ordinary Time.

Communion Antiphon

Whatever you do in word or in deed, do everything in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through him. Col 3:17

Prayer after Communion

Having been made partakers of this table of unity and charity,
we beseech your mercy, Lord,
that through the work you have given us to do
we may sustain our life on earth
and trustingly build up your Kingdom.
Through Christ our Lord.
